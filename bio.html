Matt Kirk is:

Analytical:

I've always been analytical. When I was seven, I took my parent's VCR apart. They got upset. Eventually they cooled off when I put it back together and it still worked.

My love of analysis eventually led into me studying music. While in school I would listen to to music and go home and figure out chord changes and apply them in the jazz band.

While I didn't pursue music full time in college this love of analysis continued. I studied applied mathematics and economics at the University of Washington. This was because I am fascinated by challenges that are tough to figure out. Economics is probably the toughest subject I've found.

After graduating I started my career at Parametric Portfolio Associates as a quantitative research analyst. While there I studied the effects of volatility as it relates to tax-loss harvesting. The basic idea is how higher volatility will yield better harvesting opportunities for Parametric's clients. This initial research was turned into a Russell Index after I left called CrossVol (crossvol.com).

This theme has continued throughout my career as I have carved a niche for myself doing mathematics and statistics as it relates to programming. Recently I was the Chief Scientist of SocialVolt in Kansas City. Where I was in charge of running all research and development as it relates to social media data.

Today I help business tackle hard to analyze problems with my company Modulus 7. I am in the unique position of being one of the few individuals who knows both Ruby and math at an expert level.

Focused:

I focus on the customer. When I was at UW, I typeset all my work in LaTeX. Before doing this I wrote it all by hand and my teachers would write "Can't read on the work". Instead of getting upset I decided to make it pretty and make them happy. They definitely were and my grades went up as a result.

I tend to only focus on what matters. Throughout my entire career I work towards finding a solution instead of finding the best possible one. When at Wetpaint we had a project that was running over budget and over time. I kept pushing the team to interact more with the customer and to find a solution to the problems. Eventually when we disbanded the project, we were able to restart under a much better system and interact with customers continuously instead of at the end.

Instead of worrying about what the team wants, I always focus on delivering results.

Competitive:

Competition is a fact of business. All of the companies I have worked with I have helped compete more effectively. While at Wetpaint I was able to help them grow from 0 to 10 million uniques per month using a sophisticated publishing system.

At Modulus 7 I pride myself in helping companies better position themselves in the market place and to overcome obstacles.

Self-Taught:

I am always learning more. When I was nine I read "The C Programming language". It was tough to read, although I learned a lot.

This love of learning has bled over into my career. Every single job I have had, I learn something new. At Parametric I learned C#, at Wetpaint I learned Ruby, at SocialVolt I learned Javascript / CoffeeScript. Over the course of my career I have done enterprise software to the open source software. I am good about being thrown in the deep end and coming up just fine.
